What is Atomic Power?

Atomic power, or nuclear energy, refers to the energy released during nuclear reactions. It powers cities, fuels advanced technologies, and plays a significant role in the future of energy solutions. Through processes like fission and fusion, atomic energy helps generate electricity and even treat cancer. Let's dive into some of the key aspects of atomic power!

Nuclear Fission

Nuclear fission is the process where the nucleus of an atom splits, releasing large amounts of energy, which is harnessed power plants.

Nuclear Fusion

Fusion is the process where two light atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing massive amounts of ga that fuels the sun!

Atomic Energy in Medicine

Atomic energy is used in medical applications like radiation therapy, which helps treat cancer and other serious illnesses with high precision.

Atomic Research

Ongoing research in atomic science leads to new energy solutions, space exploration advancements, and innovations in quantum technology.
